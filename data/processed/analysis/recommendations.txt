Tel Aviv, Israel | Based on 2,070 traffic segments, 27 features

## 1. MUNICIPAL DATA AS WALKABILITY PROXY
Our analysis demonstrates that municipal operational data alone explains
21.2% of traffic speed variance (Adj-R²=0.212), outperforming traffic
pattern features alone (17.8%). Combined, they explain 35.4%.
This supports the hypothesis that open municipal data captures walkability
dimensions invisible to traffic sensors or even street-level imagery.

## 5. POLICY IMPLICATIONS
  a) BUSINESS VITALITY: Restaurant density and business diversity are
     the strongest walkability signals. Zoning policies that encourage
     mixed-use ground floors in low-scoring neighborhoods (especially
     Ramat HaChayal, Rabiviim, Neve Dan) could improve walkability.

  b) STREET EVENTS: Event frequency and diversity correlate with
     walkability. Expanding cultural events to peripheral neighborhoods
     could activate underused street space.

  c) COMPLAINT-DRIVEN MAINTENANCE: Noise and infrastructure complaints
     are strong negative signals. Prioritizing complaint resolution in
     walkability-improvement target areas addresses root causes.

  d) DEMOGRAPHIC SHIFTS: Female population growth and young adult
     concentration are strong walkability indicators — not causes, but
     signals of revealed preference. Track these as early-warning
     indicators for neighborhoods gaining/losing walkability.

  e) CAR DEPENDENCY: cars_per_100_residents negatively correlates with
     walkability. Traffic calming and parking policy in high-car-ownership
     areas could shift modal balance toward walking.

## 6. COMPARISON WITH LI ET AL. (2022)
  Our municipal features successfully proxy for several visual features:
  - Business density ≈ person + bicycle pixel ratio (street vitality)
  - Noise/construction complaints ≈ truck + motorcycle ratio (discomfort)
  - Event frequency ≈ person ratio (liveliness/pleasurability)
  - Infrastructure complaints ≈ fence + wall ratio (barriers)

  NOVEL CONTRIBUTIONS not capturable by imagery:
  - Female population growth as safety proxy (revealed preference)
  - Licensed business ratio as maintenance/regulation signal
  - Evening business density as 'eyes on the street' at night
  - SES cluster as confound control (wealthier = better infrastructure)

## 7. LIMITATIONS
  - No human perception survey (ground truth); avg_speed as proxy only
  - No visual features (street imagery not acquired)
  - Neighborhood-level features assign same value to all segments in
    a neighborhood — finer granularity would improve street-level accuracy
  - Construction data only through 2022; population through 2022
  - Durbin-Watson = 1.16 suggests positive spatial autocorrelation
    (segments near each other have correlated residuals)
